White Tiger

White tigers have a rare gene that makes their fur white, yet they still maintain the characteristic stripes that other tiger species have.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera tigris tigris
  • Average Length: 2.5 - 3 meters
  • Average Lifespan: 10 - 15 years in the wild
  • Hebitat: Dense forests and grasslands of India, Nepal, and Bhutan

White Tigers, a rare color variation of Bengal Tigers, feature striking white coats and dark stripes due to a genetic abnormality. Vulnerable in the wild due to reduced camouflage, habitat loss, and hunting, they symbolize the need to conserve all tiger species despite their extreme rarity.
